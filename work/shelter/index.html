Content Strategy · Charity

Creating content governance to drive a campaign

Shelter's Renters Reform Bill campaign was delivering real change, but supporters couldn't follow the progress.

Impact at a glance

~55%Traffic increase
~40%Petition engagement growth
6 yearsTo a passed Act

While being one of the top priority campaigns, the Renters Reform Bill wasn't reaching supporters where they were in a way they understood. Digital campaign activities, updates and information were disjointed, meaning engagement had to be driven through social spend or high effort marketing.

Running user testing on existing supporters and people new to the campaign gave insights on exactly what both audience types wanted to know, such as campaign timeline and updates, key wins and easy to understand objectives. Using these users' goals, I created a content strategy that turned the campaign webpage into a hub that could answer users' questions or direct users to the key areas to take action.

The hub approach gave users an organic place to engage with the campaign, as seen with a ~55% traffic increase which resulted in a ~40% increase in the main call to action, petition signatures. The strategy also gave all the internal stakeholders and subject matter experts clear guidance on what should and shouldn't be included on the campaign pages. This ongoing content governance eventually led to the campaign completing its goal and getting real changes made in Parliament.

A measured and purposeful approach more often than not will deliver more impact than forced outreach, as your content will feel designed specifically for what your audience needs.
