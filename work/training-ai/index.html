AI & Chatbot · B2B SaaS

Turning an AI chatbot into a trusted support channel

This is what happens when an AI feature gets a proper content foundation.

Impact at a glance

93%Peak deflection rate
~65%Starting deflection rate
6 monthsSteady improvement over

A B2B SaaS platform had connected a support chatbot straight to an AI model, without a content strategy to guide it. Answers were generic, often wrong, and occasionally invented entirely, including on client-specific policy, the kind of mistake that gets escalated to the client fast.

I organised the information the chatbot was drawing from into a proper knowledge base (for better RAG retrieval), and shaped how it delivered that information: accurate, on-brand, and honest about what it didn't know. Working with the development team, I also set the rules for what the AI should and shouldn't attempt to answer on its own.

Support tickets dropped as people started getting answers they could actually trust. That freed up the support team's time, and just as importantly, it meant fewer questions escalating back to the client that should have already been answered. A good AI experience needs good conversation design behind it.
