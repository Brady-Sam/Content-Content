The symptoms

A few common ways content can hold a product back.

My audience gets frustrated and doesn't know what to do next

The information is there. It's just not structured around what someone actually needs to do at that moment.

Something about the product doesn't sound or feel right

Usually, it's not one big thing. It's the small inconsistencies adding up: tone, word choice, what gets explained and what doesn't.

The copy is inconsistent

Different people write different parts, at different times, with no shared guide or reference point.

AI writes all my content

It's fast, and it fills the page. But it doesn't know your product, your audience, or what they're trying to achieve.

We're still figuring things out

Priorities move fast, and content is usually the last thing revisited. It can fall behind the product without anyone deciding that should happen.

A quick note on AI

In content design, AI offers speed but at a real risk to quality. In the content industry (like many others) the value comes from the professionals handling the tools. This can mean using AI to work faster while an expert eye catches the weaker outputs. Or, taking all that UX thinking and decision-making to build robust content systems that deliver quality from the source.

At Content Content, AI is the virtual assistant in the trenches. I'm learning the dos and don'ts in real time, like everyone else. As it stands, it helps me work through the "middle" part of work: faster drafting, testing, refining, spelling... The problem at the start, the judgement at the end, plus the context wrapping it all together, is still me. You'd notice if it wasn't.

FAQs

Do I need content design?

Yes. Your digital product and services will be easier to understand, and deliver a better experience when the content and information are treated as part of the design process.

What is content design, and how's it different from copywriting?

The skills overlap, but a copywriter will craft compelling copy to say what your business wants to say. Content design takes what your users need to know and delivers that information in the best way for them to use your services.

Do you only work with startups?

My focus is on startups. They're often underserved when it comes to quality product content, and that's the gap I built this practice to work in. That said, I work with businesses of all sizes, scale-ups, NGOs, larger SaaS teams, wherever the same problem shows up.

We already have a designer or copywriter, why do we need this too?

Content and UX/product designers are complementary forces, by collaborating on a task each role can focus on their specifics. The same applies when working with copywriters, staying aligned on brand voice and tone so all marketing communications sound like the same business as the product.

What do you need from me to get started?

Not much to begin with. Every project starts with a call, so we can talk through your product, your goals, and where things are getting stuck. From there, I'll ask for access to the product, any existing research or feedback, and someone on your team who can answer questions and make decisions along the way. If some of that doesn't exist yet, that's fine, we work with what's there.

Do you offer one-off projects or ongoing support?

Both, we can scope your exact needs together and find a working pattern that suits your goals.
