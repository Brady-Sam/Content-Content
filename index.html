How I can help

01

The words in your product

Every product makes promises through its words. Button labels, error messages, onboarding flows, empty states. If any of it is confusing, people leave. I write the copy that gets people where they need to go, and makes sure your product keeps its promises.

  • UX writing and microcopy
  • Error and empty states
  • Onboarding and activation flows
  • Conversational and AI interface copy

02

How your team works with content

Content design is more useful as a way of working than as a one-off deliverable. I help teams build it in: the principles, the systems, the habits that make good content the default.

  • Voice and tone guidelines
  • Content principles and frameworks
  • Content modelling
  • Embedded content design practice

03

Visibility and performance

Content that works for people should also work for the business. I connect the craft to the numbers: search visibility, conversion, and copy that earns its place on the page.

  • SEO content and structure
  • Conversion copy
  • A/B testing and iteration
  • Content audits and plain language

A note on AI

Everyone is being told they need it. Not everyone needs it in the same way. I use AI in my own work and I can help you figure out where it genuinely fits in yours. No hype, no fear, no promise that a chatbot will replace your content team. Just an honest look at what it can and can't do for you right now.

The philosophy

Everyone else is promising perfection. I promise content that does its job, so your customers can get on with theirs.

Ten years of asking the awkward question.

I'm Sam Brady, a senior content designer based in London (shortly, Barcelona). I've spent a decade being the person in the room who asks "but what does this actually say to the people who have to use it?" — at a housing charity, at a global relocation platform, and across a range of agency and freelance projects.

At Benivo I built an AI support chatbot from scratch — not the technology, the content. The intent architecture, the conversation flows, the plain language knowledge base it draws from, and the feedback loop that keeps it improving. It now handles around 75% of inbound queries without a human. That is what content design as a practice looks like.

Before that, at Shelter, I worked on campaigns and content that contributed to the Renters Reform Bill. Good content in the right place can do real things.

Content Content is my independent practice. The name is intentional: it's about content, and it's about making content teams feel good about theirs. I work with product and design teams who want the words in their product to be an asset, not an afterthought.
