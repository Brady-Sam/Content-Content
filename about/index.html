About

An origin story

10+ years working in tech, across product strategy, management and content design, I came to an unconfirmed conclusion: content design mainly appears across two broad industries, public services such as GOV.UK, and charities, and established SaaS companies. There will be many exceptions to this rule, but the newer companies aren't getting the benefits of the discipline. It's something to look into later, or they survive without it and leave the work at the bottom of a to-do list, usually picked up by UX designers.

Principle

Startups are built from a vision. A lot of work has gone into shaping and articulating it into something tangible, convincing investors, staff, and ultimately users to believe in the service. Content Content's goal is to make sure that vision is communicated clearly throughout the whole service.

We're talking about practice

Why a practice? Most businesses aren't skipping content design on purpose, they just don't know it's an option yet. A practice means I can bring the work directly, in an accessible way. It also keeps the focus on the craft, not the headcount, so Content Content can move as fast as the businesses it works with.

Scope

Startups are the focus, but not the limit. If the same problem shows up in scale-ups, charities, and larger SaaS teams too, Content Content is available to help there as well.

The human in the loop

I'm Sam, the person behind Content Content. After 10 years in London, I relocated to Barcelona, for all the reasons you can imagine. It was under the Catalan sun that I decided to take the same risk my audience did, and build something for myself. If you run a startup, or have any recommendations for the city, I want to talk to you immediately.
